1 Logging In

Open the system in your browser:

http://localhost:3000/login
Admin Credentials
Email: admin@whodesir.com
Password: password

After logging in, you'll see the admin Dashboard with contractor stats and a sidebar for navigation.

2 Adding a New Contractor

1

Go to Dashboard → Click "+ Add Contractor"

Or navigate to /contractors/new

2

Fill in the contractor details

Name: Their full name or business name
Business Name: LLC or DBA name
Role: Select from the dropdown (Photography, Videography, Social Media, Designer, AI Automation, Web Designer, Developer, Copywriter, Motion Designer, Virtual Assistant, Marketing Specialist, Podcast Editor)
State: Their operating state
Country: Defaults to United States

3

Click "Create Contractor"

You'll be taken to their detail page where you can manage everything.

Tip: The role you select determines which legal addendum is automatically attached to their contract. For example, a photographer gets the Photography Addendum with equipment and licensing clauses.

3 Generating Contractor Login

After adding a contractor, you can create login credentials so they can access the system themselves.

1

Go to the Contractor's detail page

Click on their name from the Dashboard or Contractors list

2

In "Quick Actions" — enter their email

Type their real email address in the Contractor Email field (e.g., their Gmail)

3

Click "Generate Contractor Login"

The system creates their account and shows their email + temporary password. Copy and send these to them securely.

Important: Share credentials privately (email, text, etc.). Never post them publicly. The contractor uses the same login page at /login — the system auto-detects their role and redirects them to their own portal.

4 Creating a Statement of Work (SOW)

A SOW defines the financial and deliverable terms for each contractor.

1

Go to SOW Builder (/sow-builder)

Or click "Create SOW" from the contractor's detail page (it pre-selects them)

2

Fill in the SOW fields

Contractor: Select from dropdown
Rate Type: Hourly, Half Day, Full Day, Project Based, or Monthly Retainer
Rate ($): The dollar amount (e.g., 75.00)
Payment Schedule: Weekly, Biweekly, Monthly, Net 15, or Net 30
Start Date: When the engagement begins
End Date: Optional — leave blank for ongoing
Special Equipment: Any gear they need to bring (e.g., "Canon R5, Studio lights")
Software: Tools they use (e.g., "Adobe Lightroom, Photoshop")
Deliverables: List each deliverable (click "+ Add Deliverable" for more)

3

Click "Create SOW"

You'll be redirected back to the contractor's detail page. The SOW is now saved.

Example SOW for a Photographer:
Rate Type: Full Day | Rate: $500 | Payment: Net 30 | Deliverables: "Event photography (up to 8 hours)", "50+ edited high-res images", "Online gallery delivery within 7 days"

5 Approving the SOW

Before a contract can be assembled, the contractor's SOW must be approved. This ensures all terms are finalized before the legal agreement is generated.

1

Go to the Contractor's detail page

Click on their name from the Dashboard or Contractors list

2

Scroll to "Statements of Work"

You'll see the SOW listed with its current status (Draft)

3

Click "Approve" next to the SOW

The status changes from Draft to Approved. The contractor can see this status in their portal.

Important: You cannot assemble a contract until the SOW is approved. If you try, the system will show an error: "SOW must be approved before assembling contract."

6 Assembling the Contract

This merges the Master Agreement + Role Addendum + SOW into one final document.

1

Go to Assembled Contracts (/contracts)

You'll see the contract assembly panel on the left

2

Select the contractor from the dropdown

Choose the contractor you just set up

3

Click "Assemble Contract"

The system generates the full agreement. It appears in the preview panel on the right.

4

Review the contract

Read through the merged content. You'll see: Master Agreement clauses, your contractor's info, the SOW terms, and role-specific addenda. A "Draft Banner" reminds you this needs attorney review before signing.

7 Signing the Contract

Admin Signs First

1

On the Assembled Contracts page, select the contract

Scroll down to the "Signatures" section

2

In the "Agency Representative" box, draw your signature

Use your mouse or finger (on mobile) to sign. Click "Clear" to redo. Click "Sign" when done.

Contractor Signs

3

Send the contractor their login credentials

They log in at /login with the email and password you generated for them.

4

Contractor goes to "My Contracts" in their portal

They select the contract, review the full agreement, and sign in the "Your Signature" box.

5

Once both sign, the contract status changes to "active"

The agreement is now fully executed.

8 Contractor Self-Onboarding

Once a contractor has login access, they can upload their own onboarding documents from their portal:

  • Tax Form — W-9 (U.S.) or W-8BEN (international). Required.
  • Insurance Proof — General liability certificate. Required for on-location roles.
  • Licensing Proof — FAA Part 107, etc. Required for specialized roles.

They access this at /contractor/onboarding in their portal. You can see upload status from the admin contractor detail page under "Tax & Compliance."

Pre-Go-Live Checklist

  • Review all 20 master agreement clauses with a licensed attorney
  • Customize rate and payment terms in the SOW for each contractor
  • Generate login credentials and share privately with each contractor
  • Confirm contractor uploads W-9/W-8BEN before first payment
  • Both parties sign the assembled contract
  • Store a copy of the signed agreement for your records
Legal Disclaimer: This system generates draft contracts. All agreements must be reviewed and approved by a licensed attorney before execution. IP assignment, indemnity, and worker-classification clauses carry real legal risk. Do not use these templates as-is for production legal documents without professional review.
